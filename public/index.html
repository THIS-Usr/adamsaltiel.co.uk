    Hello World

    Main content of your post. Right p 100% centre The purpose of this document is to outline a new platform which is designed specifically for the use of professionals working with individuals who seek intervention in their emotional and mental life and those individuals themselves.
    The broad aim is that of engagement both between groups of professional in the formal setting of their work, to facilitate communication in their tasks, and across disparate groups of professionals to encourage active understanding of clinical and theoretical materials in contexts that have, as yet, to evolve.
    At the same time the aim is to encourage participation of those seeking care, again in ways that have yet to evolve, but certainly through engagement with others including seekers and those giving care.
     
    One immediate reaction to my ideas is that I am thinking of a forum, i.e. nothing new. I know of some and there must be several others.
    These are self defining by criteria such as membership of other organisations and groups such as professional interests or religious affiliation.
     
    Fora are very much embedded in the history of the web a subject I haven’t made a study of.
     
    This platform would be different through careful attention to access, ownership, responsibility and participants respective roles along with the data ML (machine learning) aspect.
    Here, the general issues are those of pattern matching.
    A particular concept of note is that of bias, and how to distinguish a valuable foreground from noise. This is well known in statistics as to where to set the threshold for noise. Another aspect of the same problem is that ML can be used to avoid filtering into preset patterns established prior to the actual data presented, which is effectively bias.
    Professionals in their own field often express strong a bias in the array of concepts they deploy. The issue here is that in expressing that bias other significant patterns are missed, or, to put it another way, the time it takes for the significance of other patterns to emerge is greater than it need be.
    Looking further at the possibilities of ML in relation to data external third parties may, with consent, also take an interest. Something which potentially could be revenue producing.
    There are other immediate differences. Existing forums evolve their own moderating and selection criteria. These are, by definition, narrow and exclusive.
    The broad front of the NHS has had little success with such forums, although they try, precisely because it is too broad. 
    Mixed up in this is a confusion of loyalty, affiliation and identity.
     
    I am going to try a geographic analogy:Oakland, Berkeley, San Francisco.
    Imagine I am in San Francisco and someone wants to know how to get here. It is Different instructions are needed which depend on an origin in Oakland or Berkeley. It also matters where in each location is the start or end point. Or perhaps we might decide to meet elsewhere?
    These are some of the possibilities of transformation of contextual knowledge.
    There is a role for this on the professional side of the equation, and that is the argument I am going to try to sharpen up and put to professional groups including tertiary worker stakeholders.
